(God Of War) 5*

God Of War was released on march 22nd 2005 Editor: Sony Computer Entertainment Developer: SCE Santa Monica Studio

this was the day that launched a very sucessful franchise that was soon to come. The player directs Kratos in a "third person" view. The viewing angles are composed of fixed shots or pre-calculated tracking allowing a high visibility of the action and ensuring a quasi-cinematographic realization. Though focused on action, the game also includes platform and exploration phases. The game is extremely dynamic, the action is frenetic: it is possible to fight up to ten enemies at the same time, to grab them, to project them in the air, realizing devastating combos . Once Kratos has sufficiently weakened a monster (eg a cyclops ), it can complete it by executing a bloody Quick Time Event that requires pressing the right keys at the right time. By default Kratos is equipped with Chaos Blades , two large blades each attached to a chain, themselves "welded" on the skin of its forearms. During the game, the gods of Olympus come in person to give him new weapons and "divine powers" which allow him to vary his attacks and which can be improved. Kratos also receives the ability to swim indefinitely underwater thanks to Poseidon's Trident . After defeating his enemies, or opening some coffers, Kratos absorbs orbs that allow him to recover points of life (green orbs), points of magic (orbs), or experience (red orbs).
